The Emperor Penguin

The tallest and heaviest of all living penguin species

Emperor penguin family sharing a moment.


The Emperor penguin is undoubtedly one of the most studied, photographed and scientifically analyzed penguins.

This certainly is because the astonishing trips they make to reproduce and their unparalleled sacrifices they do to preserve the species.

Among all 17 species, the Emperor is the largest penguin. Their thick complexion is due to a fat deposit of 1.2 in that help them keep their body temperature and the layers of rigid waterproof feathers that assist them to stay dry and warm. Otherwise, they could not survive in the freezing conditions of the Antarctica.
